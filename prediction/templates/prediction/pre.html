Yogurt

Yogurt is a great dairy choice for reducing your risk for diabetes. A long-term study found that a daily serving of yogurt was linked to an 18%  lower risk of developing type 2 diabetes.

Garlic

For its tiny size and low calorie count, garlic is incredibly nutritious. Many studies determine garlic is a proven healthy option for people living with diabetes.

Broccoli

Broccoli is one of the most nutritious vegetables around. One study found that consuming broccoli sprouts led to a 10% reduction in blood glucose in people with diabetes.

Oats

Oats are widely considered to be less likely to cause spikes and dips in blood sugar levels. Oats also contain B-glucans, which improve insulin sensitivity and help maintain glycemic control.
